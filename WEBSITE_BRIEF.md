# Website Rebuild Brief v2

## REFERENCE SITE
Study https://www.aisle3.com/ for design inspiration. Key elements to take from Aisle3:
- Clean, confident, tons of whitespace
- Warm cream/beige base background
- Bold typography with plenty of breathing room
- Sections feel spacious, not cramped
- Copy is conversational and human — not corporate jargon
- Smooth scroll animations
- The feeling: "These people are good at what they do and they know it"
- DO NOT copy their layout literally — take the vibe, not the wireframe

## COLOR PALETTE (FINAL)
| Token | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Cream | `#EDE8E0` | Page background, base |
| Navy | `#1E3A5F` | Primary text, headings, dark sections |
| Hunter Green | `#355E3B` | Secondary accents, some section backgrounds, service cards |
| Burnt Orange | `#CC5500` | POP color — CTAs, eyebrows, highlights, links. Use boldly but not everywhere |
| Sand | `#C4A882` | Muted warm accent, dividers, subtle backgrounds |
| Gunmetal | `#6B7B8D` | Muted text, secondary labels, meta info |
| White | `#FFFFFF` | Cards, contrast areas |

### Color Rules
- Cream is the dominant base — most of the page should feel warm and open
- Navy for all primary text and headings
- Burnt Orange is the eye-catcher — buttons, eyebrow labels, key stats. Should feel special, not overused
- Hunter Green for secondary sections (CTA bands, dark cards, alternating sections)
- Sand and Gunmetal are supporting players — borders, muted text, subtle accents
- NO gold anywhere

## TYPOGRAPHY
- Headings: DM Serif Display (serif, editorial feel)
- Body: Inter (clean sans-serif)
- Keep the pairing — it's working

- The site should feel like walking into a well-designed office — warm, confident, curated
