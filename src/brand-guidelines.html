03 Logo

The Stonecutter wordmark

The primary logo is a typographic wordmark: STONECUTTER set in Cormorant Garamond with wide letter-spacing (0.2em), with NYC stacked beneath in Hanken Grotesk at smaller scale and wider tracking. No icon. The name is the brand.

Logo Variations

Stonecutter NYC
Primary — on Cream
Stonecutter NYC
Reversed — on Navy
S/C
Monogram — Favicon & Icon

Clearspace

Minimum clearspace equals the height of the "S" in STONECUTTER on all four sides. Never crowd the wordmark. Give it room to breathe.

Logo Don'ts

  • Don't stretch or distort the wordmark in any direction
  • Don't place it on busy backgrounds without a clear zone
  • Don't change the typeface — Cormorant Garamond only
  • Don't add drop shadows, outlines, or decorative effects
  • Don't use the monogram as the primary logo when the full wordmark fits
  • Don't recolor the logo outside the approved palette (navy or cream only)

File Formats

SVG — Preferred for all digital use. Scales infinitely without quality loss.
PNG — Transparent background version for documents, presentations, email.

09 Marker Underline

The signature element

The marker underline is the one signature design element of the Stonecutter brand. A hand-drawn stroke appears beneath a single emotionally loaded word in a headline. It communicates authenticity and draws the eye to what matters most.

Every client came
through a referral.

The marker highlights the most loaded word in the sentence

Usage Rules

  • Use on ONE word per headline maximum — never two or more
  • Only on the most emotionally loaded word in the sentence
  • Color: Hunter Green tint — not plum, not navy
  • Never animate the marker stroke
  • Use SVG or PNG marker stroke image, not CSS text-decoration underline
  • Scale to match the word width exactly — no overflow, no clipping

The marker is a restraint play. Its power comes from rarity. The moment it appears on multiple words or in every section, it loses meaning. Use it once per page maximum.
