Clear space

Maintain a minimum clear space equal to the height of the "NYC" text around all sides of the logo marks.

Minimum size

Wordmark: 120px wide minimum. Monogram: 32px minimum. Below these sizes, legibility is compromised.

Approved backgrounds

Use only on Cream (#F4EFE6), Navy (#1B2D45), or Hunter Green (#355E3B) backgrounds. No photography or busy textures behind the logo.

Don't

Don't rotate, stretch, add drop shadows, change colors, or rearrange the wordmark and "NYC" relationship.

2 Color Palette

The palette is built around three core colors. Navy and Cream alternate as primary section backgrounds. Hunter Green is used for accent sections, buttons, and labels. Plum and Slate are supporting accents used sparingly.

4 Voice & Tone How We Sound

Confident, not boastful

We let our track record speak. Every client came through referral - that says more than any tagline could.

Direct, not corporate

We talk like real people. No jargon walls, no buzzword bingo. Clear language that respects the reader's time.

Selective, not exclusive

We're boutique by choice, not by limitation. We frame selectivity as quality assurance, not gatekeeping.

Partnership-oriented

We position ourselves alongside our clients, not above them. Language reflects collaboration - "we" over "you should."

Do

  • Use regular dashes, not em dashes
  • Write in sentence case (not Title Case)
  • Keep copy concise and human
  • Lead with results and reputation
  • Say "partner" instead of "manage"
  • Use specifics over superlatives

Don't

  • Use em dashes (they signal AI-generated copy)
  • Write in ALL CAPS (except the logo wordmark)
  • Use generic agency language ("full-service," "360")
  • List every service like a menu
  • Over-explain what Amazon is
  • Name specific client brands
