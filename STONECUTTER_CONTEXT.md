## 1. Who We Are

**Stonecutter** is a boutique Amazon management agency founded and led by **Lee Assoulin** (CEO/Visionary). We partner with growth-minded brands to scale profitably and sustainably on Amazon, combining strategic depth, operational rigor, and genuine human partnership.

We manage approximately **14 active clients** collectively generating roughly **$220 million in annual Amazon revenue**. Our model is full-service: advertising (PPC), catalog and listing optimization, fulfillment and logistics coordination, marketplace expansion, and strategic advisory.

We are not a vendor. We are a partner. We operate with a "fail together, win together" philosophy, and we reject clients that don't fit our profile, even if they're lucrative.

**Headquarters:** New York City (lee@stonecutter.nyc)
**Team Location:** Distributed. US-based leadership with a significant operations team in the Philippines, supported by BlankSpace Inc. for HR and staffing.

## 3. What We Do (Service Model)

Stonecutter provides **full-service Amazon management** (FAM) and **PPC-only** engagements for premium brands. Our core services:

### Full Account Management (FAM)
- **Advertising / PPC:** Campaign strategy, bid management, keyword research, ASIN targeting, Sponsored Products/Brands/Display, dayparting, budget optimization
- **Catalog & Listings:** Listing audits, A+ content, brand story, main/secondary image optimization, SEO, variation strategy, compliance
- **Supply Chain & Fulfillment:** Inventory monitoring, replenishment planning, shipment creation, FBA inbound management, stranded inventory resolution
- **Marketplace Operations:** Subscribe & Save management, deals/promotions scheduling, Buy Box monitoring, pricing strategy, coupon management
- **Strategic Advisory:** Monthly business reviews, performance diagnostics, competitive analysis, Prime Day/BFCM event planning, market expansion
- **Marketplace Expansion:** US, Canada, UK, EU (Germany, France, Italy, Spain), with cautious expansion only where meaningful revenue potential exists

### PPC-Only Engagements
- Standalone advertising management for brands that handle their own operations
- Typically on a minimum retainer model

### What We Don't Do
- Social media management or influencer marketing (we recommend but don't manage)
- DTC/Shopify management
- Content creation (we direct and brief, but rely on client or contracted creative teams)

## 5. How We Operate

### EOS (Entrepreneurial Operating System)
We run on EOS, with quarterly planning days (typically 2-day format: Day 1 for review/assessment, Day 2 for IDS and rock-setting). Key EOS components we use:

- **Vision/Traction Organizer (VTO):** Company-wide strategic alignment document
- **Quarterly Rocks:** 3-5 key objectives per person per quarter, completion tracked as a percentage
- **IDS (Identify, Discuss, Solve):** Structured problem-solving during EOS days
- **People Analyzer:** Team assessment tool tracking core value alignment and performance (A/B/C player ratings)
- **L10 Meetings:** Weekly leadership meetings for scorecard review, rock check-in, and issue resolution
- **Scorecard/KPIs:** Weekly metrics tracking across client accounts and internal operations

### Tools & Systems
- **ClickUp** - Task management, project tracking, SOPs (Business Plan tier)
- **Slack** - Real-time team communication (channels per client, functional channels)
- **Google Meet** - Video calls (internal and client-facing)
- **Fireflies.ai** - Meeting transcription and summarization
- **Helium 10** - Amazon keyword research, search volume analysis, competitive intelligence
- **Hector AI** - Amazon advertising analytics platform
- **AZ Seller Kit** - Repricing tool, Buy Box monitoring
- **Amazon Seller Central / Vendor Central** - Direct marketplace management
- **SQL Data Warehouse** - Custom-built by Emmanuel/Pedro for centralized data
- **N8N** - Automation workflows (Sebastian building these out)
- **Claude / AI Tools** - Actively adopted across leadership for reporting, analysis, communication
- **Loom** - Video communication for async explanations to clients and team
- **HubStaff** - Time tracking and attendance monitoring (PH team)

### Daily Operations
- **Morning Huddle:** Daily team-wide standup covering task updates, blockers, and personal development shares
- **Client Touchbases:** Regularly scheduled calls per client (weekly or bi-weekly)
- **Incubator Meetings:** Internal R&D sessions focused on AI, automation, and process innovation
- **Post-Mortems:** Conducted after operational incidents to improve processes

### Communication Standards
- Slack for quick communication, ClickUp for task-based work
- Client emails follow a direct, solution-oriented style (see Lee's communication preferences)
- Loom videos used for complex explanations
- Default to proactive communication rather than waiting for clients to ask

## 11. Workflow & Process Building Context

When building workflows, agents, or automations for Stonecutter, keep these principles in mind:

### Priorities
1. **Client-facing quality** - Anything a client sees must be polished, data-accurate, and insight-driven
2. **Operational efficiency** - Reduce manual work, especially repetitive reporting and data gathering
3. **Proactive alerting** - We want to know about problems before clients do
4. **Consistent communication** - Every client should receive the same quality of updates
5. **Data centralization** - All data should flow to/from the SQL warehouse as the single source of truth

### Common Workflow Patterns
- **Morning Huddle Prep:** Pull overnight metrics, flag anomalies, generate talking points
- **Client Touchbase Prep:** Performance summary, action item review, competitive insights
- **Monthly Business Review:** Comprehensive deck with revenue, ad performance, inventory, competitive landscape
- **Deal/Promotion Scheduling:** Identify opportunities, get approvals, schedule in Seller Central
- **Buy Box Monitoring:** Continuous price tracking, automated alerts when resellers undercut
- **Inventory Alerts:** Low stock warnings, stranded inventory flags, replenishment recommendations
- **Onboarding Automation:** New client intake to task generation in ClickUp
- **Reporting Automation:** Daily/weekly performance emails generated from SQL data

### Communication Style for Automations
When generating client-facing content, follow Lee's communication style:
- Direct and solution-oriented
- Lead with business impact, then explain the "why"
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- Always include clear next steps
- Use en-dashes (not em-dashes)
- No jargon without explanation
- No problems without proposed solutions
- Partnership language ("we" over "you" or "I")
